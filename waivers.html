Why Waivers Are Required

At USS Academy, safety is not optional — it is the foundation of every class, every range session, and every interaction we have with our students. Our waiver and registration process exists to protect you, your fellow students, our instructors, and the facilities where we train. Completing your waiver before you arrive is one of the most important steps you can take to ensure a smooth, professional, and safe firearms training experience in Orlando, Florida.

Liability Protection for All Participants

Firearms training involves inherent risks that must be acknowledged and understood before any student handles a weapon in a training environment. Our liability waivers serve as a formal acknowledgment that you understand these risks and agree to follow all safety rules, range commands, and instructor directives throughout your class or range session. This protects not only USS Academy and our instructors, but also every other student present during training. When everyone has completed and signed a waiver, the entire group benefits from a shared commitment to safety and accountability.

Florida Legal Requirements

The State of Florida requires firearms training providers to maintain proper documentation for all participants. Under Florida Statutes Chapter 790, which governs weapons and firearms, training academies must keep records of student participation, safety acknowledgments, and course completion. Our waivers are designed to meet and exceed these legal requirements. For concealed weapons permit (CWP) classes specifically, accurate registration information is essential because your course completion certificate must match your government-issued identification exactly. Any discrepancies between your waiver information and your ID can delay your CWP application with the Florida Department of Agriculture and Consumer Services.

Insurance and Facility Compliance

Every range and training facility we use requires proof that all participants have signed appropriate liability waivers before entering the premises. This is a standard requirement across all professional shooting ranges in Central Florida and throughout the state. Our general liability insurance also mandates that proper waiver documentation be collected and stored for every individual who participates in any USS Academy program, whether it is a beginner concealed carry class, an advanced tactical course, or a private range session. Without a completed waiver on file, we cannot allow any individual onto the range — no exceptions.

Digital Record Keeping and Your Privacy

USS Academy uses WaiverForever, a secure digital waiver platform, to collect and store all registration forms and liability waivers. This means your information is encrypted, safely stored in the cloud, and accessible to our administrative team when needed for verification or compliance purposes. We never share your personal information with third parties for marketing purposes. Your data is used solely for class registration, safety compliance, certificate issuance, and direct communication regarding your training. For full details on how we handle your information, please review our Privacy Policy.

What to Know Before Your Class

Whether you are attending your first concealed carry class or returning for advanced training, proper preparation makes a significant difference in your experience. Here is everything you need to know before arriving at USS Academy.

Complete Your Waiver in Advance

We strongly recommend completing your waiver and registration form at least 24 hours before your scheduled class. This allows our team to verify your information, confirm your enrollment, and prepare any materials you may need. Students who complete their waivers in advance spend less time on paperwork when they arrive and can focus entirely on learning. Walk-in waivers are available, but completing them on-site can add 10 to 15 minutes to your check-in process.

What to Bring to Class

  • Valid government-issued photo ID — Florida driver's license, state ID, passport, or military ID. Your name on the ID must match the name on your waiver exactly.
  • Proof of payment — Confirmation email, Groupon voucher, or receipt from your booking platform.
  • Pen and notepad — While not required, many students find it helpful to take notes during classroom instruction, especially during the Florida law portions of the CWP course.
  • Eye and ear protection — USS Academy provides basic eye and ear protection for range sessions. However, if you own quality shooting glasses or electronic ear protection, you are welcome to bring them.
  • Water and snacks — Some classes run several hours. Staying hydrated and energized helps you stay focused throughout the entire session.

Dress Code and Attire

Dress comfortably but appropriately for a firearms training environment. Closed-toe shoes are mandatory — sandals, flip-flops, and open-toe footwear are not permitted on any range. We recommend wearing a high-neck shirt or crew neck top, as hot brass casings can eject in unpredictable directions. Avoid low-cut shirts, loose scarves, or any clothing that could catch a spent casing. Hats with a forward-facing brim are recommended to help deflect brass. Athletic or casual wear is perfectly acceptable — there is no need to dress formally.

Arrive on Time

Please arrive at least 15 minutes before your scheduled class start time. This gives you time to check in, verify your waiver is on file, and settle in before instruction begins. Late arrivals may not be admitted to class, and no refund will be issued for missed sessions. If you are running late, call us immediately at 800-508-8772 so we can advise you on your options.

Firearm Rentals and Ammunition

You do not need to own a firearm to take a class at USS Academy. We provide rental firearms and ammunition for all CWP classes and most training courses. If you prefer to use your own firearm during range portions of the class, it must be inspected and approved by the instructor before use. All firearms must be unloaded and cased when you arrive at the facility. Never handle a firearm in the parking lot or common areas.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why do I need to sign a waiver for a concealed carry class?

Every USS Academy class that involves firearms instruction, live-fire range time, or handling of weapons requires a signed waiver. This is both a Florida legal requirement and an insurance mandate. The waiver confirms that you understand the inherent risks of firearms training, agree to follow all safety rules and instructor commands, and acknowledge our liability terms. It also collects the personal information needed to issue your course completion certificate, which is required for your CWP application with the Florida Department of Agriculture.

What are the age requirements for USS Academy classes?

For concealed weapons permit classes, you must be at least 21 years of age under Florida law (or 18 if you are an active-duty military member or veteran with an honorable discharge). For general firearms training and range sessions, participants must be at least 18 years old. Minors aged 12 and older may participate in certain supervised range activities with a parent or legal guardian present who has also signed the appropriate waiver. The parent or guardian must remain on-site for the entire duration of the minor's session. Our General Range Waiver includes a section for parental consent for minors.

Can I complete my waiver digitally, or do I need to sign a paper form?

All USS Academy waivers are completed digitally through our secure WaiverForever platform. There is no paper form to fill out. Simply select the appropriate waiver from the options above, complete the form on your phone, tablet, or computer, and submit it electronically. Your digital signature is legally binding under the Electronic Signatures in Global and National Commerce (E-SIGN) Act and the Uniform Electronic Transactions Act (UETA), which Florida has adopted. Once submitted, you will receive a confirmation email with a copy of your signed waiver for your records.

When should I complete my waiver — before class or when I arrive?

We recommend completing your waiver at least 24 hours before your scheduled class or range session. Completing it in advance speeds up your check-in process and ensures our team has time to verify your information. If you forget to complete it beforehand, you can fill it out on your phone when you arrive, but please allow an extra 10 to 15 minutes before class begins. Either way, you will not be permitted to participate in any training activity until your waiver is on file.

Which waiver do I need to fill out?

That depends on the type of class or activity you are attending. If you are a new student attending a concealed weapons permit (CWP) course, start with the Class Registration and Welcome Packet — this covers your enrollment, personal details, and liability agreement. If you are specifically registering for the CWP exam, complete the CWP Exam Registration form, which includes ID verification and certificate delivery preferences. For any range-only activities, private sessions, Bullseye Benefactor sessions, or special events, complete the General Range Waiver. If you are unsure which form you need, call us at 800-508-8772 and we will point you in the right direction.
