How to Get Your Florida Concealed Carry Permit: Step by Step

Published April 5, 2026 · USS Academy

Getting your Florida concealed carry permit is one of the most important steps you can take to exercise your Second Amendment rights responsibly. Whether you are a first-time gun owner or someone who has carried openly and now wants the legal flexibility of concealed carry, understanding the full process removes uncertainty and sets you up for success. At USS Academy, we have guided over 29,000 students through this exact journey over the past 27+ years, and this guide will walk you through every stage so you know exactly what to expect.

Florida is a shall-issue state, meaning the state must issue your concealed carry permit as long as you meet all legal requirements. That is great news for law-abiding citizens, but it also means the process has specific steps you cannot skip. Let us break them down.

Who Is Eligible for a Florida Concealed Carry Permit?

Before you begin the application process, you need to confirm you meet the eligibility requirements set by the Florida Department of Agriculture and Consumer Services (FDACS), which administers the Concealed Weapon License (CWL) program.

To qualify for a Florida concealed carry permit, you must:

  • Be at least 21 years of age (or 18 if you are an active-duty or honorably discharged member of the U.S. military)
  • Be a U.S. citizen or permanent resident alien
  • Not have a felony conviction (unless your civil rights have been restored)
  • Not have had adjudication withheld or a suspended sentence on a felony or misdemeanor crime of violence within the past three years
  • Not be a chronic or habitual user of alcohol or controlled substances
  • Not have been committed to a mental institution or adjudicated mentally defective
  • Not have a current restraining order or injunction against you
  • Demonstrate competency with a firearm through an approved training course

That last requirement is where most people start their journey, and it is the step where working with a qualified training provider makes the biggest difference. If you have a disqualifying factor and are unsure whether it applies, consult a firearms attorney before beginning the process. Our instructors are not lawyers, but we can point you to resources that help.

At USS Academy, we welcome students of all ages into our class. We have had students as young as 10 years old complete this course. Maturity matters more than age. The 21-year requirement applies to the state application — not to training. Younger students who complete the class receive a certificate they can hold and use when they are ready to apply.

Who Only Needs the Class — No Qualifying Required

Not everyone needs to complete the qualifying course of fire. If you fall into one of the following categories, the class alone may be all you need:

  • Active duty or veteran military
  • Law enforcement
  • Private security
  • Safety course graduates
  • Competitive shooters

If you fall into one of these brackets, contact us before booking so we can make sure you are registered for the right course.

The 3-Step Process to Your Florida Concealed Carry Permit

Step 1 — The Foundations Class

This is where every student starts, regardless of experience level. Before anyone steps onto the range, we make sure you have the foundation to be a safe, responsible gun owner.

In the classroom, we cover:

  • The four universal rules of firearm safety — non-negotiable for every gun owner
  • How to hold, grip, and properly handle a handgun
  • How to load and unload your magazine
  • How to recognize and clear malfunctions
  • Range etiquette — how to conduct yourself as a responsible shooter
  • An overview of Florida Statute 790 (weapons and firearms) and Florida Statute 776 (justifiable use of force)

Class runs from 9AM to 11AM.

After the classroom portion, every student gets their first live-fire experience — one rotation, typically 3 to 5 rounds. This is not your qualifying course of fire. This is your first time applying what you just learned on the range so your instructor can see how you handle the weapon in a real environment. Think of it as your practice round before the exam.

Step 2 — The Qualifying Course of Fire

The qualifying exam runs from 11:30AM to 1:30PM and is available the same day as your class.

This is the paid upgrade at $179 that moves you from student to certified.

Your instructor will run you through their chosen qualifying course of fire. You will shoot approximately three volleys. You must meet the passing score on the instructor's matrix. Once you pass, if time and resources allow, you are welcome to keep shooting.

Pass the course and you receive your Certificate of Completion. This certificate is valid for 90 days — that is your window to submit your state application.

We strongly recommend the Certificate Upgrade.

The standard certificate that comes with your exam expires in 90 days. The upgrade gives you a certificate that never expires, and the benefits go well beyond that:

  • No expiration — ever
  • License lapses? No need to redo the class. Use your certificate to reapply
  • Multi-state use — accepted in other states that honor a firearms training certificate, so you can apply for permits in those states without retaking a course
  • Lost your certificate? Call us. We reissue it
  • Backup and support from USS Academy long after your class is done

Most of our students choose the upgrade. We highly recommend it.

Step 3 — Submit Your State Application

Certificate of Completion in hand, you are ready to apply for your Florida Concealed Weapon License through the FDACS.

You will need:

  • Your Certificate of Completion from Step 2
  • A passport-quality photograph (color, taken within the last 30 days)
  • Proof of U.S. citizenship or permanent residency
  • Completed application form (available online at the FDACS website or at a regional office)
  • The application fee

The most efficient method is visiting a regional FDACS office in person — you can get fingerprinted, photographed, and submit everything in one visit. Many of our students go the same day they complete class or within the following week. We give you clear instructions on exactly where to go and what to bring so there are no surprises.

Fingerprinting is required by the state. Prints are submitted to FDLE and the FBI for a background check. The fee is typically around $42. USS Academy can direct you to fingerprinting providers in the Orlando and Central Florida area after class.

The application fee for a new Florida concealed carry permit is $97, which includes the $42 fingerprint processing fee if done at a regional office. Check the FDACS website for current pricing as fees can change.

How Long Does It Take?

The Florida Department of Agriculture website states the state has up to 75 days to process your application. However, students who follow our instructions exactly consistently receive their license in as little as 3 to 5 business days to 2 weeks from the day they apply.

Follow the process exactly as we outline it and you will be carrying legally faster than you think. Deviate from the process and expect delays.

What You Can Carry — and Where

Once you receive your Florida concealed carry permit, you are authorized to carry a concealed weapon or firearm on your person or in close proximity. Florida's permit covers handguns as well as other weapons such as electronic weapons, tear gas guns, knives, and billies.

However, there are places where carrying is prohibited even with a valid permit:

  • Any place of nuisance as defined by law
  • Police, sheriff, or highway patrol stations
  • Detention facilities, prisons, or jails
  • Courthouses
  • Polling places
  • Government meetings
  • School administration buildings and school-sponsored events (with some exceptions)
  • Career centers
  • Establishments licensed to serve alcohol where the primary purpose is on-premises alcohol consumption
  • College or university facilities
  • Inside airport terminals (past security)
  • Any posted federal buildings

Knowing where you can and cannot carry is just as important as knowing how to shoot. This is one reason our CWP class at USS Academy goes beyond the bare minimum. We want you to be legally informed, not just technically qualified.
