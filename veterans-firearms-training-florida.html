Overview

For Veterans, By Instructors Who Served

Your service gave you real handling skill, real time under pressure, and real respect for what a firearm is and what it can do. USS Academy's veterans-focused firearms training is built around that reality. We do not teach you what you already know. We help you meet Florida's specific civilian concealed weapons license requirement, sharpen skills that transfer imperfectly to civilian carry, and continue the professional development many veterans pursue after their service.

Over 27 years, USS Academy has trained thousands of veterans moving into civilian life, and many of those students have gone on to teach on our roster. Our instructor team includes United States Marine Corps veterans, active and retired law enforcement officers, and credentialed firearms instructors. Your instructor understands where you are coming from.

Why Veterans Still Take Civilian Training

Florida's Civilian CWL Requirement

Your military service gave you more firearm handling experience than most civilians will ever have. But Florida's Concealed Weapons License (CWL) requirement is specific: it requires a certified civilian training course that includes live-fire qualification under Florida Statute 790.06(2)(h). Military service, law enforcement service, and hunter-safety certifications do not automatically satisfy this requirement.

USS Academy's civilian CWP class is designed so veterans complete the state requirement efficiently, cleanly, and with a same-day state-approved certificate in hand for the FDACS application. We do not make you sit through material you already understand — but we do cover what the state requires us to cover, including Florida-specific law (Statute 776, stand your ground, castle doctrine) and civilian-context decision-making that differs from military rules of engagement.

The Veteran Classroom Experience

Instruction That Respects Your Service

Veterans in our classes are treated with the respect their service earned. Instructors do not repeat basics that every veteran already knows. Classroom time is efficient. Range time focuses on the specific skill differences between military handling and civilian concealed carry — which are real, and which matter legally and practically.

Many classes mix veterans with active and retired law enforcement officers, and the conversation in these classes tends to be richer than any other. Veterans learn from cops. Cops learn from veterans. First-time civilian shooters learn from both. It is the kind of environment only a training program with 27 years and 29,000+ students can create.

Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Do veterans need firearms training?

Many veterans complete USS Academy programs specifically to meet Florida's civilian concealed weapons license training requirement. Your military service gives you handling skill. The state still requires a certified civilian training course for the CWL.

Do you offer a veterans discount?

USS Academy honors veterans and first responders with service pricing on select programs. Contact 407-305-8335 for current veteran pricing.

Can I use my GI Bill for firearms instructor training?

USS Academy's Tier 6 Professional Development program does not currently accept GI Bill directly. Veterans pursuing instructor credentials should contact our Professional Development team to discuss pathway options.

What classes are most common for veterans?

CWP class for the Florida Concealed Weapons License, advanced skill development, tactical training, and Tier 6 professional development for veterans pursuing careers as firearms instructors or range safety officers.

Do you train together with active and retired law enforcement?

Yes. Our classes regularly include veterans alongside active and retired law enforcement, and the mix of backgrounds often enriches the class conversation.

Are your instructors veterans?

Many are. Our roster includes United States Marine Corps veterans, active and retired law enforcement officers, and instructors with law enforcement firearms instructor credentials. Your instructor understands where you are coming from.
