The CWP Class Orlando Trusts

If you have made the decision to carry concealed in the state of Florida, the first step is real — not theoretical — training. USS Academy's CWP class in Orlando is the original training program that has prepared more than 29,000 Florida residents to legally carry a concealed firearm, confidently handle real scenarios, and pass the state-required proficiency standard. Every class is taught in person, includes live-fire range work, and ends with a state-approved Certificate of Completion that meets the requirement under Florida Statute 790.06(2)(h).

This is not a classroom-only online checkbox. This is not a "shoot-one-round-and-sign-here" rubber-stamp course. USS Academy's CWP training is delivered by certified Law Enforcement Firearms Instructors, U.S. Marine Corps instructors, NRA Instructors, USCCA Instructors, and Florida Class K Instructors. Our students leave the range knowing how to load, draw, aim, fire, clear malfunctions, reholster, and — just as important — understand the legal weight of every decision they make while carrying a firearm under Florida law.

What the CWP Class Covers

Florida's concealed weapons permit requirement is clear on paper but has very real implications the moment you carry live. USS Academy's Orlando CWP class is built to give you both: legal knowledge and physical capability. In one training day you walk through:

  • Florida law — Statute 790 (firearms), Statute 776 (justifiable use of force), reciprocity, carry zones, prohibited places, duty to retreat, stand your ground, castle doctrine.
  • Firearm safety — the four universal rules, safe storage, transport in vehicles, family-in-home considerations.
  • Mechanics and handling — loading and unloading, grip, stance, sight alignment, sight picture, trigger control, reholstering.
  • Live-fire qualification — range safety brief, instructor-supervised live-fire, state-required minimum competency demonstration, paper-target proof for your permit file.
  • Home defense, scenario awareness, and de-escalation — real-world thinking, not TV thinking.
  • Application roadmap — how to submit your permit application to the Florida Department of Agriculture and Consumer Services once you have your Certificate of Completion in hand.

Who This Class Is For

If you are a Florida resident (or eligible non-resident) 21 or older with the legal right to possess a firearm, the CWP class is the correct starting point. USS Academy's class is built for every learner — from first-time gun owners who have never held a pistol, to seasoned shooters who simply need the certified course to complete their application, to military veterans and law-enforcement officers whose backgrounds already give them handling skill but who want to meet the formal state requirement.

We specifically welcome women who are stepping into training for the first time, and we regularly run sessions with a majority of female students led by instructors who understand that learning to carry is a personal, and often deeply emotional, decision — not just a transaction.

Every student who successfully completes the class receives a state-approved Certificate of Completion the same day, signed by the instructor and dated. You use this certificate to complete your Florida Concealed Weapons Permit application directly with the Florida Department of Agriculture and Consumer Services (FDACS) or at a county tax collector's office. USS Academy's instructors will walk you through the exact submission steps at the end of class — the paperwork is straightforward, and we want you completing the process with confidence.

Florida CWP Requirements — Short Version

To apply for a Florida Concealed Weapons Permit you must be at least 21 years of age (or 18 with qualifying military service), a U.S. citizen or lawful permanent resident, legally able to possess a firearm, and able to demonstrate competency with a firearm. Demonstrating competency requires a certified training course that includes live-fire instruction — which is exactly what the USS Academy Orlando CWP class provides. Your Certificate of Completion is accepted by FDACS across all 67 Florida counties.

For the full official FDACS requirements, visit fdacs.gov. For the legal framework, the relevant Florida statutes are Chapter 790 and Chapter 776. USS Academy's CWP class covers all of this in plain English during the classroom session.
